Delivering successful workshops requires being intentional

Delivering successful workshops requires being intentional about the business or organizational outcome, the participants needed to achieve that goal, and how much time is required. I am certified to teach the LUMA system of human-centered design's thirty-six methods, and certified as an facilitator leveraging these methods. Any given workshop may include a variety of interactive group activity and presentation, depending on the goal.

Ensuring that your workshop participants use their valuable time well, I will work with you each step of the way to....

Plan
  • Identify workshop outcomes and key participants.
  • Define workshop journey based on the outcomes..
  • Create participant communications.
Execute
  • Prepare the space (virtual or physical).
  • Manage the arc of the conversation to assure participants have equal voices.
  • Guide the workshop toward objectives and adapt to changes as needed.
Analyze
  • Communicate accomplishments to key stakeholders and participants.
  • Analyze artifacts and document key moments in the workshop.

Examples of impactful workshops are below. Tailoring the workshop to your specific needs will create the most impact for your organization or product.

Ad Hoc Workshops

Competitive War Gaming

Scenario Planning

Map possible scenarios driven by technology, policy, or geopolitical shifts impacting your product.

Define what tangible and intangible value is exchange between actors in your industry, or your organization.

Value Stream Analysis

Quickly understand where value is leaked, blocked, or flows in organizational or customer workflows.
